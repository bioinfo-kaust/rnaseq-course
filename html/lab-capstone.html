Part 1: Recap — Course Dataset (GSE136366)

Biological Background

TDP-43 (encoded by the TARDBP gene) is a highly conserved RNA-binding protein with critical roles in RNA metabolism, including pre-mRNA splicing, mRNA stability, and the regulation of non-coding RNAs. Loss-of-function or cytoplasmic mislocalization of TDP-43 is a defining pathological hallmark of two devastating neurodegenerative diseases: Amyotrophic Lateral Sclerosis (ALS) and Frontotemporal Lobar Degeneration (FTLD).

GSE136366 uses RNA-seq to systematically characterize the transcriptomic changes that occur when TDP-43 is knocked down in human cells. By comparing knockdown samples to controls, the study reveals which genes and pathways are directly or indirectly regulated by TDP-43, providing mechanistic insight into how its loss contributes to neurodegeneration.

3. Check the MultiQC Report

Open the MultiQC report generated by nf-core/rnaseq (in nfcore_rnaseq/results/multiqc/). Record the following metrics for each sample and flag any outliers:

  • Uniquely mapped reads (%) — expect >70% for a good human RNA-seq run
  • Duplication rate (%) — high duplication may indicate poor library complexity
  • Adapter content — should be near zero after trimming
  • % Assigned reads (featureCounts) — how many reads map to annotated genes
  • RSeQC read distribution — are reads predominantly in exons?
What to look for

Any sample with mapping rate below 60%, duplication above 80%, or dramatically different read counts compared to its replicates should be noted. Consider whether it should be excluded from downstream analysis and document your reasoning.

5. Functional Enrichment Analysis

Perform three types of enrichment analysis to understand the biological processes affected by TDP-43 knockdown:

  • GO Biological Process ORA — over-representation analysis on the set of significant upregulated genes (padj < 0.05, LFC > 1)
  • KEGG pathway ORA — identify dysregulated KEGG pathways among significant genes
  • GSEA with Hallmark gene sets — gene set enrichment analysis using the full ranked gene list (ranked by log2FoldChange * -log10(padj))
Hint 
library(clusterProfiler)
library(org.Hs.eg.db)
library(msigdbr)
library(enrichplot)

# Load DESeq2 results produced by nf-core/differentialabundance
res_df <- read.delim(
    "nfcore_diffab/results/deseq2/KD_vs_CTRL.deseq2.results.tsv",
    stringsAsFactors = FALSE
)
# Column names: gene_id, baseMean, log2FoldChange, lfcSE, stat, pvalue, padj, gene_name
res_df$gene <- res_df$gene_name   # use gene symbols for enrichment

# Convert gene symbols to Entrez IDs
sig_up <- subset(res_df, padj < 0.05 & log2FoldChange > 1)$gene
entrez_up <- bitr(sig_up, fromType="SYMBOL", toType="ENTREZID",
                   OrgDb=org.Hs.eg.db)$ENTREZID

# GO BP ORA
go_res <- enrichGO(gene=entrez_up, OrgDb=org.Hs.eg.db,
                    ont="BP", pAdjustMethod="BH",
                    pvalueCutoff=0.05, readable=TRUE)
pdf("enrichment/go_enrichment_dotplot.pdf", width=9, height=8)
dotplot(go_res, showCategory=20) +
    ggtitle("GO Biological Process: Upregulated in TDP-43 KD")
dev.off()

# KEGG pathway ORA
kegg_res <- enrichKEGG(gene=entrez_up, organism="hsa",
                        pAdjustMethod="BH", pvalueCutoff=0.05)
kegg_res <- setReadable(kegg_res, OrgDb=org.Hs.eg.db, keyType="ENTREZID")
pdf("enrichment/kegg_dotplot.pdf", width=9, height=8)
dotplot(kegg_res, showCategory=20) +
    ggtitle("KEGG Pathway ORA: Upregulated in TDP-43 KD")
dev.off()

# Hallmark GSEA
hallmark_sets <- msigdbr(species="Homo sapiens", category="H")

ranked <- res_df$log2FoldChange * -log10(res_df$padj + 1e-300)
names(ranked) <- res_df$gene
ranked <- sort(ranked[!is.na(ranked)], decreasing=TRUE)

gsea_res <- GSEA(ranked, TERM2GENE=hallmark_sets[, c("gs_name","gene_symbol")],
                  pvalueCutoff=0.05, verbose=FALSE)
pdf("enrichment/gsea_hallmark_barplot.pdf", width=10, height=8)
barplot(gsea_res, showCategory=20) +
    ggtitle("GSEA Hallmark Gene Sets: TDP-43 KD vs Control")
dev.off()

6. Biological Interpretation

This is the most important step. Review all your outputs and answer the interpretation questions in Part 3 below. Write your answers as a short summary (~1 page). Think about what the data tells you biologically, not just statistically.

Tip: Cross-reference the literature

TDP-43 biology is well-studied. Look up the original GSE136366 publication (Rot et al., 2017 or the associated paper) and compare your top DEGs and enriched pathways to what was reported. Does your independent analysis recover the same findings?

Part 3: Interpretation Questions

Answer these questions in writing as part of your capstone deliverable. Aim for 2–4 sentences per question — be specific and use numbers from your analysis.

Question 1: Scope of Differential Expression

How many genes are significantly differentially expressed (padj < 0.05 and |log2FC| > 1)? How many are upregulated and how many are downregulated? The human genome encodes approximately 20,000 protein-coding genes — what fraction is affected by TDP-43 loss?

Question 2: Biological Processes Affected

Based on your GO and GSEA results, what biological processes are most significantly enriched among genes dysregulated by TDP-43 knockdown? Are these processes consistent with the known biology of TDP-43 as an RNA-binding protein involved in splicing and RNA stability? Cite at least one process that you expected and one that surprised you.

Question 3: Top Hallmark Gene Set

Which Hallmark gene set from MSigDB shows the highest Normalized Enrichment Score (NES) in your GSEA results? Is it enriched (positive NES) or depleted (negative NES)? What does this suggest about the cellular state when TDP-43 is absent?

Question 4: Sample Outliers

Do all samples cluster as expected in the PCA plot (i.e., KD samples together, control samples together)? If any sample appears to be an outlier, describe what you observe and explain how you would decide whether to exclude it from the analysis. What are the consequences of including vs. excluding an outlier replicate?

Question 5: GO vs. KEGG vs. GSEA

Compare the results from your three enrichment analyses. Do GO ORA, KEGG ORA, and GSEA identify the same biological processes, or do they highlight different aspects of TDP-43 biology? Which method do you find most informative for this dataset and why? Consider the differences in what each method tests (gene sets vs. pathway databases, ORA vs. ranked list).

Run StringTie on the STAR-aligned BAM files to perform transcript assembly and identify potentially novel transcripts or alternative isoforms that are not present in the reference annotation. Compare StringTie's assembled transcript count to the number of annotated transcripts in the GTF.

Extension 3: Inspect the TARDBP Gene Itself

TDP-43 is encoded by the TARDBP gene. Plot its normalized read counts across all samples to verify that the knockdown is working as expected. Is the reduction in TARDBP expression statistically significant? How consistent is the knockdown across replicates? A successful siRNA knockdown should show a substantial and consistent reduction in expression.

Hint
